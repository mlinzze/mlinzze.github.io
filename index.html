Manuel Linsenmeier

PhD candidate in Environmental Economics

London School of Economics and Political Science

I am a PhD candidate in Environmental Economics at the London School of Economics and Political Science, where I am affiliated with the Department of Geography and Environment and the Grantham Research Institute on Climate Change and the Environment.

With my research, I contribute to our fundamental understanding of the relationship between human activity and the natural environment. I also hope to contribute to better policies of managing this relationship. My main research interests are climate change and sustainable development. I am particularly interested in adaptation to climate change and in the political economy of carbon pricing.

In my PhD thesis I focus on the impacts of climate change and how we can adapt to them. Specifically, in two current working papers I examine the costs and benefits of temperature variability. For this purpose, I use economic theory and apply econometric methods to a wide range of data sources including national accounts, results of climate models, and satellite data.

My research is motivated by its policy relevance. In summer 2021 I have been working on carbon pricing at the Research Department of the International Monetary Fund. Before starting my PhD, I worked several years for the thinktank adelphi, designing, coordinating, and contributing to research and consultancy projects on a variety of environmental topics.

Having a multidisciplinary background I aim to build bridges between the natural and social sciences. I am also a passionate teacher of economics and environmental sciences. I have been designing and teaching courses on environmental economics, development and trade, sustainable development, and environmental change.

Research interests:

  • Environmental economics
  • Economics of climate change
  • Climate econometrics
  • Development economics
  • Economic geography
