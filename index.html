Manuel Linsenmeier

PhD candidate in Environmental Economics

London School of Economics and Political Science

I am a PhD candidate in Environmental Economics at the London School of Economics and Political Science, where I am affiliated with the Department of Geography and Environment and the Grantham Research Institute on Climate Change and the Environment. From April to August 2022 I am visiting the School of International and Public Affairs at Columbia University.

My main research interests are climate change and sustainable development. I am particularly interested in the costs of climate change, adaptation to climate change, and in the political economy of climate change mitigation. I am also interested in the the effect of weather and climate on ecological systems and the use and benefits of weather forecasts.

I have previously conducted research at the International Monetary Fund. I have also worked at the thinktank adelphi, designing, coordinating, and contributing to research and consultancy projects on a variety of environmental topics. In addition to my research and policy advice, I am a passionate teacher of economics and environmental sciences.

I am co-organising the Shifting Landscapes 2022 conference for PhD students and early-career researchers. The conference will take place on June 16th and 17th in London. The program is now available on the conference website.
